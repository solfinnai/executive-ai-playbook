THE AUDIT NOBODY DOES

Most AI consultants will tell you to audit your workflows and find where AI can help. That's backwards.

Here's the audit that actually changes how you think:

The Subtraction Audit

Instead of asking "what can AI do for us?", ask this:

"If my AI could do everything perfectly, what would I still need a human for?"

Write down every answer. Be ruthlessly honest. Not "well, someone needs to approve the social posts" (AI can draft them, you review in 30 seconds). Real human-only work:

  • Deciding the company's strategic direction
  • Building relationships with key partners
  • Making judgment calls with incomplete information
  • Creative direction and taste
  • Being the face of the brand
  • Navigating ambiguous ethical situations

Everything that's NOT on that list is a candidate for AI. Everything.

How to Run It

Block 90 minutes. No phones. Just you and a whiteboard (or a doc).

Step 1: List every recurring task in your organization. All of them. From "schedule team meetings" to "analyze quarterly revenue." Be specific. Not "marketing" but "draft LinkedIn post" and "research competitor pricing" and "create monthly analytics report."

You should have 50-100 tasks.

Step 2: For each task, ask: "Would a well-trained AI agent do this adequately?"

Not perfectly. Adequately. Because "adequate done instantly" beats "perfect done in three days" nearly every time.

Mark each task: AUTOMATE (AI does it), AUGMENT (AI does 80%, human refines), or HUMAN (requires real human judgment).

Step 3: Look at the AUTOMATE column. Count the hours per week those tasks consume across your team. Multiply by your average hourly cost. That's the money you're leaving on the table every single week.

In my experience, the number shocks people. It's usually 40-60% of total team hours.

Step 4: Now look at the HUMAN column. That's your actual company. That's what you should be hiring for, managing, optimizing. Everything else is infrastructure, and infrastructure should be automated.

The Uncomfortable Truth

When I run this exercise with executives, the most uncomfortable moment isn't seeing how much can be automated. It's realizing that several roles in their organization exist primarily to do work that AI can do better.

I'm not saying fire everyone. I'm saying redeploy them. Move your people from tasks AI handles (formatting reports, triaging email, scheduling, data entry, first-draft writing) to work AI can't do (strategy, relationships, creative direction, judgment).

The companies that make this shift win. The companies that protect their old org charts because "that's how we've always done it" lose. Slowly at first, then all at once.

THE ONE-PERSON PRODUCT LAUNCH

This is the chapter that will break your brain.

I'm going to show you how I shipped a complete product in a single evening. Not a prototype. Not a mockup. A real product, with a real website, that accepts real payments. In one sitting.

What is Vibe Coding?

Vibe coding is building software by describing what you want in plain English and having AI generate the code. You don't need to know Python, JavaScript, HTML, or any programming language. You need to know what you want.

Think of it like being an architect. You don't lay bricks. You draw the blueprint. AI lays the bricks.

The Evening Build

Here's what happened on March 12, 2026. A real evening. Not a hypothetical.

7:00 PM — I decided to build a landing page for Executive Intelligence, my AI consulting company. I had no website, no product page, no payment system.

7:15 PM — I told my AI agent (his name is Sol) what I wanted. A professional single-page website with: hero section, credibility bar, three service offerings, a how-it-works section, an about section with stats, a playbook purchase section, and a call-to-action footer.

7:45 PM — Sol built the entire site. HTML, CSS, everything. I opened it in my browser and reviewed it. It looked professional. Not perfect. But 90% there.

8:00 PM — I refined. "Make the hero bigger. Change the pricing on the services. Add the GK Labs link in the footer." Sol made each change in seconds.

8:15 PM — Separately, Sol had written this guide you're reading right now. Seven chapters. Real content. Not filler.

8:30 PM — I set up a Gumroad account (5 minutes). Created the product listing. Set the price.

8:45 PM — Deployed the website to Vercel (free hosting). One command in the terminal.

9:00 PM — A complete business was live. Website, digital product, payment processing, booking system for consulting calls. Two hours, start to finish.

A traditional company would have: hired a web agency ($5-15K), waited 4-6 weeks, gone through 3 rounds of revisions, hired a copywriter ($2-5K), set up Stripe integration (a developer for a week), and launched two months later.

I did it in two hours for $0 in additional costs.

How You Can Do This

You don't need my AI agent (yet). You need:

  • Claude or ChatGPT (free or $20/month)
  • A clear idea of what you want to build
  • The willingness to describe it and iterate

Here's the prompt framework:

"Build me a [type of product/page]. It should include [list specific sections or features]. The tone should be [describe]. The target audience is [describe]. Use [any specific design preferences]."

Then iterate: "Change the headline to X. Move the pricing section above the testimonials. Make the CTA button blue."

Each cycle takes 30 seconds. You'll have something ready to ship within an hour.

The Key Insight

The expensive thing was never the code. It was never the design. It was never the hosting. The expensive thing was the TIME and COORDINATION required to get all those pieces to come together.

AI collapsed that time from months to hours. And the coordination from a team of 5-10 people to one person talking to their computer.

This changes everything about how businesses can be started, tested, and scaled. A single person can now launch 10 products in the time it used to take to launch one. And if 9 of them fail, the one that works can be scaled with the same lean infrastructure.

THE AI OPERATIONS STACK

This is the chapter where I show you inside my actual company. Not theory. The real tools, agents, and workflows running 0N1 Corp right now.

Meet Sol Finn

Sol Finn is my AI operations partner. Not a chatbot. An agent.

The difference: a chatbot waits for you to ask a question and then answers it. An agent has access to your tools, your data, your systems. It takes actions on your behalf, within rules you define.

Sol runs on a framework called OpenClaw, connected to Claude (Anthropic's most powerful AI model). Here's what Sol can do:

  • Read and write files on my computer
  • Read and respond to emails
  • Manage my calendar
  • Search the web and fetch content
  • Run code and terminal commands
  • Send and receive messages on Telegram, WhatsApp
  • Scan markets and generate trading signals
  • Build websites, apps, and documents
  • Maintain a persistent memory across conversations

Sol wakes up every session, reads his memory files, and knows exactly who he is, who I am, what we're working on, and what needs to happen next.

I don't manage Sol with a to-do list. I built a system where Sol manages himself:

HEARTBEAT system: Every 15-30 minutes, Sol checks in. What's the plan for today? What's on track? What's blocked? Are there emails that need attention? Any trading signals above threshold? He does this automatically. If something needs my attention, he alerts me. If everything's fine, he stays quiet.

3-Layer Memory:

  • Layer 1: Knowledge graph. Durable facts about my world organized by entity (companies, people, projects).
  • Layer 2: Daily notes. A timeline of what happened each day.
  • Layer 3: Tacit knowledge. Patterns about how I operate. My preferences, communication style, what motivates me, what drains me.

This means Sol gets better over time. He learns that I hate em dashes in writing. He learns that I shut down when overwhelmed and need the priority list cut to 3 items. He learns my voice so content he drafts sounds like me, not like AI.

You don't need to start with the full stack. Start with one agent doing one job:

Each level builds on the last. Start where you are. Scale as you get comfortable.

The Handoff Model

The key to making this work is clean handoffs between humans and AI. Here's how we structure them:

AI Initiates → Human Decides → AI Executes

Example: Sol scans the market and finds a high-conviction trading signal. Sol presents the signal to me with reasoning. I say yes or no. Sol executes (or doesn't).

Example: Sol drafts a LinkedIn post based on something we shipped today. I review it, adjust three words, approve. Sol publishes.

Example: Sol summarizes an incoming partnership inquiry email. I decide if it's worth pursuing and give a one-line direction ("interested, suggest a call next week"). Sol drafts the full response.

In each case, the human work is the 10-second judgment call. The AI work is the 30-minute preparation and execution.

THE AUTONOMOUS REVENUE MACHINE

This is the chapter most people will skip to first. And that's fine. Because this is where it gets real.

An AI-native company doesn't just use AI to save time. It uses AI to generate revenue while the founder sleeps.

Revenue Stream 1: Automated Trading

Here's a real story. I had a friend who built a trading bot for Kalshi (a prediction market). The bot trades 15-minute binary contracts on whether BTC, ETH, SOL, and XRP will go up or down. It uses 24 weighted signals, 10 safety gates, and runs automatically every 15 minutes.

Starting capital: $150.
Current value after 2 weeks: $1,071.
That's a 7x return.

Key insights from the system:

  • Cross-market consensus (what Polymarket thinks) is the single strongest signal
  • Mean-reversion beats trend-following on 15-minute crypto
  • Certain hours have 0% win rate. The bot simply doesn't trade during those hours.
  • Flat bet sizing ($25 max) outperformed Kelly criterion scaling
  • The bot has a kill switch: if daily losses hit $150, it stops. No exceptions.

This isn't "get rich quick." The bot has a 52-65% win rate depending on the asset. It wins by having a slight edge and executing it hundreds of times with perfect discipline. No emotions. No FOMO. No revenge trading.

And it runs on a laptop. While the founder sleeps.
